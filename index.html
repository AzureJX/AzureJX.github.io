About

Where hardware meets code

I'm a second-year ECE student at Carnegie Mellon, currently spending my summer at Siemens Energy building a robot that inspects the inside of generator stators — tight, dark, low-texture spaces where GPS and clean camera tracking don't exist. Outside of that, my projects tend to circle the same question: how do you get a signal you can trust out of a sensor, a circuit, or a chip, and turn it into something a machine can act on? That's shown up in FPGA-accelerated particle physics pipelines, rocket telemetry boards, and a computer-vision lithography rig — different domains, same instinct for instrumentation.
